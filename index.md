# Turx Computer Systems Laboratory

An open-source systems research laboratory dedicated to building modular operating systems, experimental platforms, and advanced algorithms for modern x86-64 architectures.

---

## Our Mission & Research Focus

At **Turx Lab**, we believe that significant systems research can be conducted with a lean and focused approach. We utilize simulation, emulation, and bare-metal debugging to explore low-level computer engineering without requiring massive server infrastructures.

### Core Research Areas

- **Operating Systems Architecture:** Designing and implementing **Turx OS**, a highly modular x86-64 microkernel-based operating system.
- **Experimental Algorithms:** Researching novel data structures and optimizations specifically tailored for low-level system performance.
- **Systems Emulation & Bare-Metal Testing:** Utilizing hypervisors, advanced QEMU environments, and GDB to test and debug kernel code directly on physical and emulated hardware.

---

## Infrastructure & Lab Setup

Our development and testing environments are optimized for high-efficiency Ar-Ge processes within a minimalist physical footprint:

- **Virtualization & Emulation:** Advanced x86-64 system simulations using QEMU and Bochs.
- **Hardware-Level Debugging:** Remote GDB integration for stepping through kernel executions.
- **Bare-Metal Testing:** Real-world hardware evaluations using specialized single-board systems or mini PCs connected via serial/USB setups.
